Overview

This section outlines the compliance methods as specified in the North Carolina Existing Building Code. Compliance methods are essential for ensuring that existing buildings meet safety and performance standards.

Compliance Methods

Prescriptive Compliance Method

The Prescriptive Compliance Method is a straightforward approach where existing buildings must comply with specific requirements outlined in Chapter 4 of the NCEBC. This method:

  • Provides clear requirements by building element
  • Is suitable for most common renovation projects
  • Doesn't require complex analysis or calculations
  • May be more restrictive for certain historic structures

Work Area Compliance Method

The Work Area Compliance Method addresses compliance based on the classification of work and the extent of the work area. It categorizes work as:

  • Level 1 Alterations: Removal and replacement of existing materials with like materials
  • Level 2 Alterations: Reconfiguration of space, addition or elimination of doors/windows
  • Level 3 Alterations: Work area exceeding 50% of the building area
  • Change of Occupancy: Changing the use of a space
  • Additions: New construction connected to an existing building
  • Historic Buildings: Special provisions for designated historic buildings

Performance Compliance Method

The Performance Compliance Method allows flexibility by using a scoring system to evaluate safety features. This method:

  • Uses a numerical scoring system
  • Evaluates fire safety, means of egress, and general safety
  • Allows trade-offs between building systems
  • Requires thorough documentation and calculations
  • Is suitable for complex or unusual buildings
