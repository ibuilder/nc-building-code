Building Permits

Building permits are required for most construction activities in North Carolina. The permitting process ensures that planned construction adheres to the current building code.

When Permits Are Required

Generally, permits are required for:

  • New construction
  • Additions to existing structures
  • Renovations that involve structural changes
  • Changes in occupancy classification
  • Electrical, plumbing, and mechanical system modifications

Enforcement Authority

In North Carolina, enforcement authority typically resides with local jurisdiction building officials. These officials have the authority to:

  • Review plans and specifications
  • Issue permits
  • Conduct inspections
  • Issue notices of violation
  • Stop work orders
  • Certificates of occupancy

Inspection Process

The typical inspection process includes:

  • Foundation inspection
  • Framing inspection
  • Electrical rough-in inspection
  • Plumbing rough-in inspection
  • Mechanical rough-in inspection
  • Insulation inspection
  • Final inspection

Appeals Process

When there is a disagreement about code interpretation or application, an appeals process is available. Appeals may be filed with:

  1. Local Board of Appeals (if established)
  2. North Carolina Building Code Council
  3. Courts (as a last resort)
